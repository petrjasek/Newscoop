The original Campsite concept was developed by developed by Micz Flor
and Sava Tatić for Center for Advanced Media--Prague (CAMP).

It was developed in collaboration with Aleksandar Brajanoski from
Transitions Online, the first site to go live on Campsite in late 2000.
MDLF originally founded CAMP (Center for Advanced Media–Prague) in 1998
as its new-media arm. Campcaster is now being developed by an
international community of developers and designers led by Sourcefabric
(formerly Campware).

The original funding was provided by Open Society Institute's Internet
Program. Initial coding was done by DNT Cluj and then by Recognos.
Campsite code was brought to its first public release by a team of
CAMP New Media Fellows. Developer teams at DNT-Cluj and Recognos,
software companies based in Cluj, Romania, provided the initial coding.
